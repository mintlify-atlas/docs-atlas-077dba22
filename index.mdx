ESPHome JK-BMS is an open-source ESPHome external component that bridges Jikong Battery Management Systems (JK-BMS) to your smart home. Install it on an ESP32 or ESP8266, connect via UART-TTL or Bluetooth Low Energy, and instantly surface dozens of live sensor readings — cell voltages, temperatures, state of charge, protection thresholds, and more — directly into Home Assistant or any MQTT broker.

## What you can do

ESPHome JK-BMS exposes the full JK-BMS data surface to ESPHome, enabling you to:

- Read **per-cell voltages** (up to 32 cells), pack voltage, current, and power
- Monitor **temperatures** from the power tube and up to four external sensors
- Track **state of charge**, capacity remaining, charging cycles, and total runtime
- Control **charging**, **discharging**, and **cell balancing** switches remotely
- Read and write **protection thresholds** — overvoltage, undervoltage, overcurrent, and temperature limits — directly from the BMS
- Monitor **NEEY/Heltec Smart Active Balancers** via a dedicated BLE component

## Getting started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install ESPHome">
    Make sure you have ESPHome 2025.7.0 or higher installed.

    ```bash
    pip3 install esphome
    ```
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add the external component">
    Reference this component in your ESPHome YAML configuration.

    ```yaml
    external_components:
      - source: github://syssi/esphome-jk-bms@main
    ```
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your connection method">
    Pick [UART-TTL](/connection/uart-ttl) for a wired connection via the BMS GPS port, or [BLE](/connection/ble) for wireless monitoring. Follow the relevant guide to wire up your hardware and configure your YAML.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Flash and connect">
    Run `esphome run your-config.yaml` to compile, flash, and open the live log. You should immediately see cell voltages and BMS status appearing in the output.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  ESPHome 2025.7.0 or higher is required. All JK-BMS models with software version 6.0 or higher are supported over UART-TTL. For BLE, select the correct `protocol_version` based on your hardware version.
</Note>
